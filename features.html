Not all search engines are created equal - Information retrieval made easy

Legal Research is the “process of identifying and retrieving information necessary to support legal decision making”. It is the analysis of the issue which is then concluded with the interpretation and application of the relevant information.

In this era of digitization, India has been struggling with an effective and efficient legal research portal. Except the conventional ones, the majority of judges, attorneys, researchers, scholars, law students and librarians have moved online for legal research. 33.9% of the attorneys frequently initiate their research by using Google, which is the most generic search engine. Legal stakeholders are wasting their precious time in scouting for relevant case laws online.

Considering these practical issues and aiming to reduce the time involved in legal research, LegitQuest has come out with iSearch, a legal research tool which is powered by Artificial Intelligence. It has a database of more than one million records.

iSearch provides you with

  • The most relevant case law, statute or regulation in the minimum time.
  • Customized search options by Case Name, Citation and Judge’s name.
  • Relevant filter options which assist the user by narrowing to the most relevant result.

World’s Only 1 Click judgment insight system

Across the globe, legal information and case laws are available in raw form and constitute the most complex and unstructured data. This data is collated by publishers and online legal portals to provide inputs for better analyses.

One-third of the attorneys’ time is spent in collecting primary and secondary sources of data and then reading and analyzing the ever-growing case law precedents.

With the use of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, LegitQuest has come out with a revolutionary feature called iDRAF. It is the world’s only segregation system which provides the user with Decision, Reasoning, Arguments and the Facts of the case law at a click. This tool contains tabs that take the user to specific portions of the judgment within seconds, making the job of analyzing the case more effective, efficient and faster.

Features of iDRAF

  • It will help you reduce the time in analysing the case laws;
  • It culls out the issues, arguments, reasoning, statutes, case cited and the decision of case laws of the Supreme Court of India since 1950.
  • iDRAF has been thoroughly checked for quality, reliability and utility by a team of expert legal researchers.

Intelligence through Visuals

Attorneys always want to find out the latest cases which work in their favour, as the most recent cases will have the settled law. This product feature has the power to help you find those latest cases that can make your research robust.

Knowing the treatment of a case can provide you with ample understanding about the relevance of the case you may decide to research. But right now, it is hard to extract how many times a case has been followed, relied upon, distinguished, overruled, etc. in the several judgements where it has been cited.

With our revolutionary feature called iGraphics, you would be able to see the treatment of case laws condensed in the form of figures and charts. iGraphics summarises the treatment as overruled, distinguished, followed and relied upon case laws. The complex data is presented on a single canvas for easy reference and understanding. iGraphics is trained to understand the treatment parameters which are checked for quality and reliability by a team of experts. So, now you do not have to waste your precious time in reading the entire judgment only to find that it has been overruled.

Features of iGraphics

  • It presents the treatment in easily identifiable colours which helps you understand the most unstructured data in a convenient way.
  • The treatment parameters are presented under the following buckets - overruled, approved, cited, referred and distinguished.
  • Clicking on the treatment parameters will take you to those cases where it has been cited.
  • This also helps you refine the case judgements for your research.
